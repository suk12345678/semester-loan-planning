Visualizing Interest Accrual

The calculator shows you how interest grows over time:

  • Freshman year loans accrue interest for 4+ years before repayment
  • Senior year loans only accrue for 6 months before repayment
  • This is why borrowing less early on saves more money

Example

$5,000 borrowed freshman year at 5% interest:

  • Interest during 4 years of school: ~$1,000
  • If capitalized, you'll pay interest on that $1,000 too

$5,000 borrowed senior year at 5% interest:

  • Interest during 6-month grace period: ~$125

The 80% Rule Calculator

The calculator automatically checks if your total debt exceeds 80% of your expected salary.

Green Zone

Total debt is less than 80% of expected salary. You should be able to manage repayment comfortably.

Yellow Zone

Total debt is 80-100% of expected salary. Repayment will be tight but manageable.

Red Zone

Total debt exceeds 100% of expected salary. You may struggle with repayment. Consider reducing costs or increasing expected income.

Adjusting Your Plan

If the calculator shows you're borrowing too much, try:

  • Reducing housing costs
  • Increasing work hours
  • Applying for more scholarships
  • Choosing a less expensive school
  • Graduating early

The calculator lets you adjust these variables in real-time to see the impact.
