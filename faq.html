Semester Loan Planning FAQ

Common Questions About Semester Loan Planning

Get answers about how much to borrow and how to plan your borrowing across all four years.

How much should I borrow each semester?

Only borrow what you need after exhausting other funding sources:

(Tuition + Fees + Housing + Books) - (Scholarships + Grants + Family + Savings + Work) = Amount to Borrow

Avoid borrowing for non-essential expenses.

What are the federal loan limits?

Dependent undergraduates:

  • Freshman: $5,500/year
  • Sophomore: $6,500/year
  • Junior/Senior: $7,500/year

Independent undergraduates:

  • Freshman: $9,500/year
  • Sophomore: $10,500/year
  • Junior/Senior: $12,500/year

Graduate students: $20,500/year

Should I borrow the maximum amount offered?

No! Just because you're offered a certain amount doesn't mean you should take it all. Borrow only what you actually need. Every dollar borrowed accrues interest.

Can I adjust my loan amount after accepting it?

Yes! You can reduce or cancel your loan amount anytime before disbursement by contacting your financial aid office. You can also return funds within 120 days of disbursement without paying interest.

What if I need more money mid-semester?

Contact your financial aid office, but first explore alternatives:

  • Emergency student loans from your school
  • Payment plans
  • Part-time work
  • Reducing expenses

Avoid private loans if possible.

Should I take loans for summer classes?

Only if necessary for graduation timeline. Summer loans count toward your annual limit. Consider:

  • Working full-time in summer instead
  • Taking fewer classes
  • Using savings

Summer borrowing should be strategic, not automatic.

How do I avoid overborrowing?

  • Create a semester budget
  • Live frugally
  • Work part-time
  • Choose affordable housing
  • Buy used textbooks
  • Minimize discretionary spending
  • Reassess loan needs each semester
  • Track total debt and projected payments
