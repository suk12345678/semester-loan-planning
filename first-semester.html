Your First Semester

What to expect when borrowing for the first time

First-Time Borrower Requirements

Before your first federal student loan can be disbursed, you must complete two things:

1. Entrance Counseling

This is a mandatory online session that explains:

  • What you're agreeing to
  • How interest works
  • Your repayment obligations
  • Your rights and responsibilities

It takes about 30 minutes. Do it at StudentAid.gov.

2. Master Promissory Note (MPN)

This is the legal document where you promise to repay your loans.

  • One MPN covers all your Direct Loans for up to 10 years
  • You only sign it once (unless you change schools)
  • Also completed at StudentAid.gov

Do This Early

Don't wait until the last minute. If you don't complete entrance counseling and the MPN, your loan won't disburse, and you'll have a gap in funding.

The 30-Day Delay

If this is your very first federal student loan, there's a mandatory waiting period:

  • Your first disbursement cannot happen until 30 days after your enrollment period begins
  • This is a federal requirement to give you time to reconsider

What This Means

If classes start August 20:

  • Your first disbursement can't happen before September 19
  • You'll need to cover expenses for the first month from other sources
  • Plan ahead for books, housing deposits, meal plans, etc.

Subsidized vs Unsubsidized

You'll likely be offered both types. Here's the difference:

Subsidized Loans

  • Based on financial need
  • Government pays interest while you're in school
  • Lower annual limits
  • Always take these first

Unsubsidized Loans

  • Not based on need
  • Interest accrues from day one
  • Higher annual limits
  • Only take if you need more than subsidized covers

Your First Disbursement

When It Happens

Typically within the first 10 days of the semester (plus the 30-day delay for first-time borrowers).

Where It Goes

  1. Directly to your school
  2. School applies it to tuition and fees
  3. Then to housing and meal plan (if applicable)
  4. Leftover amount is refunded to you

Getting Your Refund

Set up direct deposit in your student portal. It's much faster than waiting for a paper check.

Common First-Semester Mistakes

1. Not Completing Requirements on Time

Missing entrance counseling or MPN deadlines delays your disbursement.

2. Accepting the Full Amount Without Thinking

You can reduce your loan amount. Don't borrow more than you need.

3. Spending Refunds on Non-Essentials

That refund is borrowed money. Use it for education expenses only.

4. Not Tracking What You Borrowed

Write down how much you borrowed. You'll need to know this for future planning.

5. Ignoring Interest Accrual

If you have unsubsidized loans, interest is accruing right now. Even small payments help.

Setting Yourself Up for Success

Create a Budget

Track every dollar. Know where your money is going.

Look for Ways to Reduce Costs

  • Buy used textbooks or rent them
  • Cook instead of eating out
  • Use free campus resources
  • Avoid unnecessary purchases

Consider Working Part-Time

Even 10 hours a week can reduce how much you need to borrow.

Apply for Scholarships

Keep applying every semester. Small scholarships add up.

What to Expect Next Semester

Good news: Future semesters are easier.

  • No 30-day delay (that's only for first-time borrowers)
  • MPN is already signed (covers you for 10 years)
  • You know what to expect

But you'll still need to:

  • Accept or reduce your loan amount each semester
  • Track your total debt
  • Make smart borrowing decisions
