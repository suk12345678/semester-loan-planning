ðŸŽ“ Lessons from Jordan's Experience

  1. Dream schools aren't worth crushing debt - Your degree matters more than the name
  2. Private loans are dangerous - Higher interest, fewer protections
  3. Consider your expected salary - Film grads don't earn enough to justify $100K debt
  4. NYC is expensive - Living costs add up fast
  5. Cheaper alternatives exist - USC, Chapman, even Ohio State have film programs
  6. You can't discharge student loans in bankruptcy - This debt follows you forever

ðŸ” The Bottom Line

Jordan's story is unfortunately common. Thousands of students take on massive debt for prestigious schools, only to find themselves financially crippled for decades.

Before you borrow $100K for college, ask yourself:

  • What will I realistically earn after graduation?
  • Can I afford $1,000+/month in loan payments?
  • Will this degree actually lead to higher earnings?
  • Are there cheaper schools with similar outcomes?
  • Am I willing to sacrifice my 20s and 30s to debt?

Remember: You can always move to NYC after graduation. You don't need to pay $332,000 to live there for 4 years.
